Catullus: The Neo Poet

Catullus of Rome

Gaius Valerius Catullus was a latin poet of the late Roman Republic, writing in the neoteric style of poetry. That is, he fancied writing about personal endeavours rather than classical and political heroes. He was more relateable to others in this sense. His surviving works are still heavily influencial on poetry and other art forms.

After his rediscovery in the Late Middle Ages, he was again admired for his explicit style. Although, never popular in schools, he is still appreciated in graduate programs around the world.

Understanding the Life of a Poet

As one of my personally favorite poets of the neo-romantic genre, Catullus' life is inspirational in the values instilled in him through family life as well as personal ventures.
