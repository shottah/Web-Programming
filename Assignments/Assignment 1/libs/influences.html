Catullus: The Neo Poet

The Style of Catullus

Catullus often wrote in elegiac couplets, common in love poems. A great part of his poetry shows strong and occasionally erratic emotions, especially in regard to Lesbia, the personification of his lover within his poems. He portrays this dynamic as a requited lover to someone whom had many suitors, but still manages to demonstrate great sense of humor.
