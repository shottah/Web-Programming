Catullus: The Neo Poet

Life's Work

Catullus' collection of 116 Carmina is hailed as his magnum opus. The volume is split into three parts: poems, short poems of varying meters, and epigrams.

Carmina LXXII

I adored you - not in the way a girl would be,
By a whole crowd of suitors, rather more
As a father loves his sons and sons-in-law.
I really know you now; therefore, despite
My fiercer burning, you're still, in my sight,
A cheaper and more trivial thing by far.
How canthis be? You ask. Because a scar
Like this compels your lover under stress,
To love you more, but respect you less.
